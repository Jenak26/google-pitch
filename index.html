The Why The Missions

I don't just learn.

I build solutions.

For me, technology isn't about memorizing syntax. It's about building something that works. That's why this website itself is my creative pitch. It’s a real-world example of my philosophy: find a problem, get your hands dirty, and build a solution that helps people.

From Frustration to
a Framework.

I've always been fascinated by how technology can solve problems. But as I've navigated my studies at VIT, I've noticed a common pattern: we have incredibly bright students learning powerful skills, but often in isolation. The most exciting breakthroughs in my own projects have come from late-night discussions with friends, bouncing ideas off each other and tackling a problem from different angles.

That's when I realized the biggest opportunity on our campus isn't just about learning more code; it's about creating a space where these conversations can happen at scale. My passion is to build that space—a community where students from all departments can connect, share their skills, and turn their academic knowledge into practical, collaborative projects.

The Three Missions

A clear plan for real impact.

Mission 01: The AI Launchpad

AI is changing the world, but many students feel it's too complex to touch. This mission is about breaking down that barrier. We'll run workshops on Google's AI tools like Gemini and Teachable Machine, showing how anyone—from artists to biologists—can leverage AI to innovate in their field.

Mission 02: Code & Chai Sessions

Forget boring lectures. These are weekly, informal meetups where students bring their laptops and problems. We'll work together, share ideas, and foster peer-to-peer learning in a relaxed environment.

Mission 03: The 'Zero to Hero' Showcase

A platform to celebrate the journey, not just the destination. We'll feature projects from absolute beginners, showing their first lines of code to their final product. It's about making tech accessible and celebrating growth.
